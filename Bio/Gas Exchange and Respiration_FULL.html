Gas Exchange & Respiration

This chapter differentiates between Gas Exchange (physical movement of air and diffusion of gases) and Respiration (chemical breakdown of nutrients for energy).

1. The Human Gas Exchange System

Air enters via the nose/mouth and travels down to the lungs. The path is: Trachea → Bronchi → Bronchioles → Alveoli.

PartStructure & Function
Trachea (Windpipe)Tube connecting throat to lungs. Contains C-shaped rings of cartilage to keep it open (prevent collapse during inhalation).
BronchiThe trachea splits into two bronchi (one for each lung).
BronchiolesSmaller branches of the bronchi that end at the alveoli.
AlveoliTiny air sacs where Gas Exchange occurs.
Rib CageBone structure protecting the lungs.
Intercostal MusclesMuscles between ribs involved in ventilation.
DiaphragmSheet of muscle and tissue at the bottom of the thorax.
Goblet Cells & CiliaGoblet cells make mucus (traps dust/bacteria). Cilia (hairs) beat to move mucus up and out.

2. Ventilation (Breathing)

Pressure & Volume: Gas moves from High Pressure to Low Pressure. To breathe in, we must lower the pressure inside the lungs by increasing the volume.

3. Gas Exchange at the Alveoli

Oxygen diffuses from Alveoli → Blood. Carbon Dioxide diffuses from Blood → Alveoli. This happens by Diffusion.

Adaptations of Alveoli:

  • Large Surface Area: Millions of alveoli increase the rate of diffusion.
  • Thin Walls: Only one cell thick (short diffusion distance).
  • Good Blood Supply: Surrounded by capillaries to maintain a high concentration gradient.
  • Moist Lining: Gases dissolve in liquid to diffuse efficiently.

4. Respiration

Exam Warning:
Do not confuse "Respiration" with "Breathing". Respiration is a chemical reaction in cells (mitochondria) to release energy. Breathing is just moving air.

A. Aerobic Respiration

Breakdown of glucose using oxygen. Releases a large amount of energy.

  • Word Equation: Glucose + Oxygen → Carbon Dioxide + Water + Energy
  • Chemical Equation: C6H12O6 + 6O2 → 6CO2 + 6H2O

B. Anaerobic Respiration

Breakdown of glucose without oxygen. Releases much less energy.

1. In Muscles (Humans):

  • Glucose → Lactic Acid + Energy
  • Oxygen Debt: Lactic acid builds up in muscles causing fatigue. After exercise, you breathe fast (pant) to get extra oxygen to break down this lactic acid in the liver.

2. In Yeast (Fermentation):

  • Glucose → Alcohol (Ethanol) + Carbon Dioxide + Energy
  • Used in brewing (alcohol) and baking (CO2 makes bread rise).

6. Smoking and Health

Tobacco smoke contains harmful chemicals:

  • Tar: Causes cancer (carcinogen). Damages cilia, causing mucus buildup → Smoker's Cough → Bronchitis.
  • Nicotine: Addictive. Increases heart rate and blood pressure.
  • Carbon Monoxide (CO): Binds to haemoglobin permanently, reducing oxygen carrying capacity.
  • Emphysema: Smoke damages alveoli walls, reducing surface area. Causes severe breathlessness.
