Q29
Genetics — Inheritance & Chromosomes
A cell contains 46 chromosomes. The cell divides by meiosis.
How many chromosomes are in each of the cells produced? A 11 B 23 C 46 D 92
1 mark
⚡ High distractor rate
Mark Scheme Answer
B
Meiosis halves the chromosome number. 46 ÷ 2 = 23. Students commonly choose A (dividing by 4) confusing with second division products, or C thinking chromosomes are preserved.
Q33
Hormones — Insulin and Blood Glucose
A person eats a large meal. Which sequence shows what happens to blood glucose? A blood glucose rises → glucagon secreted → liver converts glycogen to glucose B blood glucose rises → insulin secreted → liver converts glucose to glycogen C blood glucose falls → insulin secreted → liver converts glycogen to glucose D blood glucose falls → glucagon secreted → liver converts glucose to glycogen
1 mark
⚡ High distractor rate
Mark Scheme Answer
B
After a meal, blood glucose rises. The pancreas secretes insulin (not glucagon). Insulin causes the liver to convert excess glucose into glycogen for storage. Glucagon does the opposite — it raises blood glucose when it drops.
Q37
Ecology — Population & Food Webs
In a food web, owls eat rabbits and rabbits eat grass. If a disease kills most of the rabbits, what is the most likely effect? A grass decreases; owls increase B grass decreases; owls decrease C grass increases; owls decrease D grass increases; owls increase
1 mark
📊 Data interpretation
Mark Scheme Answer
C
Fewer rabbits → less grass eaten → grass population increases. Fewer rabbits → owls have less food → owl population decreases. Students often get confused about the indirect effect on grass.
Q39
DNA & Protein Synthesis
Which statement about DNA is correct? A DNA contains the sugar ribose B DNA is made of amino acids C DNA is found in the nucleus of cells D DNA leaves the nucleus during protein synthesis
1 mark
⚡ Hard concept
Mark Scheme Answer
C
DNA is found in the nucleus. DNA contains deoxyribose (not ribose — that's RNA). DNA is made of nucleotides (not amino acids). It is mRNA, not DNA, that leaves the nucleus during protein synthesis.
June 2024 (v1) — 0610/11
Q31
Transport in Plants — Transpiration Rate
Which conditions would produce the fastest rate of transpiration? A high humidity, high temperature, high wind speed B high humidity, low temperature, low wind speed C low humidity, high temperature, high wind speed D low humidity, low temperature, low wind speed
1 mark
⚡ Hard concept
Mark Scheme Answer
C
Transpiration is fastest when: humidity is LOW (large concentration gradient for water vapour), temperature is HIGH (more kinetic energy, stomata open wider), wind speed is HIGH (removes water vapour quickly). Students often think high humidity increases transpiration — the opposite is true.
Q35
Respiration — Aerobic vs Anaerobic
Which row correctly describes aerobic and anaerobic respiration in mammals? A Aerobic: produces lactic acid. Anaerobic: uses oxygen B Aerobic: uses oxygen, produces CO₂ and water. Anaerobic: produces lactic acid only C Aerobic: produces ethanol. Anaerobic: produces lactic acid and CO₂ D Aerobic: produces ethanol and CO₂. Anaerobic: uses less energy
1 mark
⚡ Hard concept
Mark Scheme Answer
B
In mammals: Aerobic = glucose + oxygen → CO₂ + water + ATP. Anaerobic = glucose → lactic acid (no CO₂, no ethanol in mammals — ethanol is produced by yeast). Students confuse yeast anaerobic respiration with mammalian anaerobic.
Q38
Natural Selection & Evolution
A population of beetles lives on dark tree bark. Birds eat the beetles. Some beetles have a mutation giving them dark colouring. What will happen over many generations? A All beetles will have mutations B Dark beetles will increase in number C Light beetles will increase in number D The bird population will increase
1 mark
📊 Data interpretation
Mark Scheme Answer
B
Dark beetles are better camouflaged on dark bark → less likely to be eaten by birds → more likely to survive and reproduce → pass dark alleles to offspring → frequency of dark colouring increases over generations. This is natural selection in action.
November 2024 (v2) — 0610/12
Q30
Circulatory System — Heart Structure
The left ventricle has a thicker muscular wall than the right ventricle. Why? A It pumps blood at a lower pressure B It pumps blood to the lungs only C It pumps blood to the whole body D It receives blood from the body
1 mark
⚡ Hard concept
Mark Scheme Answer
C
The left ventricle pumps oxygenated blood to the entire body via the aorta — a much longer distance requiring higher pressure. The right ventricle only pumps to the nearby lungs (short distance, lower pressure needed). Thicker muscle = stronger contraction = higher pressure.
Q36
Excretion — Kidney Function
Which row shows what happens to glucose and urea between the glomerulus and the collecting duct? A Glucose: filtered then reabsorbed. Urea: filtered, not reabsorbed B Glucose: not filtered. Urea: filtered then reabsorbed C Glucose: filtered, not reabsorbed. Urea: filtered then reabsorbed D Glucose: filtered then reabsorbed. Urea: not filtered
1 mark
⚡ High distractor rate
Mark Scheme Answer
A
Glucose: filtered out at glomerulus → completely reabsorbed back into blood (useful substance, none lost in urine). Urea: filtered out at glomerulus → NOT reabsorbed → leaves in urine (waste product). Urea is a small molecule that passes through but stays as waste.

Q2(c)
Ecology — Food Web Explanation
Context: A food web includes: algae → small fish → large fish (coral grouper) → sharks; algae → sea urchins → turtles; sea cucumbers → turtles.

Overharvesting of the large fish (coral grouper) would cause the turtle population to decrease. Using the food web, explain why the turtle population would decrease.
3 marks
🔴 Chain reasoning required
Mark Scheme — Any 3 from:
✓Sharks eat more turtles (because there are fewer large fish for sharks to eat)
✓Because there are fewer large fish / coral grouper for sharks to eat
✓Population of small fish increases (less predation by large fish)
✓More small fish eating algae / algal population decreases
✓Less food (sea cucumbers) for turtles / turtles only eat sea cucumbers
⚠ Must follow food web logic. Simply saying "less food" without a chain earns 0. Must link large fish → sharks → turtles OR large fish → small fish → algae → sea cucumbers → turtles.
Q3(b)(i)
Photosynthesis — Gas Exchange Adaptation
Context: A cross-section of a leaf shows parts labelled A (spongy mesophyll), B (stomata), C (guard cells), D (air spaces).

Explain how the parts labelled A, B, C and D in the diagram adapt the leaf for gas exchange during photosynthesis.
4 marks
🔴 4-mark explain
Mark Scheme — Any 4 from (must mention each label):
ASpongy mesophyll: orientation of cells allows gases to move easily to palisade mesophyll
BStomata: hole / opening that lets named gases (CO₂ / O₂) enter or leave the leaf
CGuard cells: open and close stomata / control stomatal size
DAir spaces: allow gases to move (easily) / allow gases to reach palisade mesophyll by diffusion
✓Gases move by diffusion (if not already credited)
⚠ Must explicitly link each labelled structure to its role. Describing a structure without linking to gas exchange gets 0.
Q7(b)
Human Impact — Effects of Deforestation
Outline the undesirable effects of deforestation.
3 marks
🔴 Students frequently lose marks
Mark Scheme — Any 3 from:
✓Reducing biodiversity
✓Disruption of food webs and food chains
✓Habitat destruction / extinction of species
✓Loss of soil / soil erosion / flooding
✓Increased carbon dioxide in atmosphere → enhanced greenhouse effect / global warming / climate change
✓Change in rainfall patterns (AVP)
⚠ "Kills animals" alone = 0. Must state the consequence e.g. "habitat destruction leads to loss of species / extinction." Link deforestation clearly to each effect.
Q8(c)
Respiration — Rate Calculation
Context: Results table showing glucose concentration 0.4 mol/dm³ takes 60 seconds to produce 1 cm³ of gas.

The rate of gas production can be calculated using: rate = 1 ÷ time

Calculate the rate of gas production for the 0.4 mol/dm³ glucose solution. Give your answer to two decimal places.
2 marks
📊 Calculation
Mark Scheme
MP1Correct calculation: 1 ÷ 60 = 0.016666... cm³ per s
MP2Correct rounding to 2 decimal places: 0.02 cm³ per s
⚠ MP2 carries ecf from MP1. If MP1 is wrong but MP2 is correctly rounded from it, MP2 is still awarded. Units must be cm³ per s.

Q2(c)(i)
Enzymes — Comparing Enzyme Activity Graphs
Context: Graph shows two enzymes (A and B) with rate of activity vs temperature. Both are active between 40–45°C. Enzyme A is active at lower temperatures, Enzyme B at higher.

Describe and compare the activity of enzymes A and B shown in the graph. (4 marks)
4 marks
📊 Graph comparison
Mark Scheme — Any 4 from:
1The (rate of) activity of both enzymes increases then decreases with increasing temperature
2Both enzymes are active between 40 and 45/46°C
3Enzyme A is active at lower temperatures / Enzyme B is active at higher temperatures
4The optimum temperature of A is lower than B (or reverse argument)
5Enzyme A is denatured at a lower temperature than B (or reverse argument)
6Correct data quote for either enzyme supporting any marking point
⚠ Enzyme A active range: 0–44/45/46°C. Enzyme B active range: 39/40/41–89/90/91°C. Data quotes earn a separate mark — always quote values from graph.
Q3(a)(i)
Cells & Diffusion — Surface Area Calculation
Context: Agar blocks of different sizes were placed in acid. A student measures how far the yellow region (acid front) has moved in each block after a set time.

The surface area to volume ratio of a cube with 5mm sides = ?
Calculate the surface area to volume ratio of a cube with sides of 5 mm. Give your answer to the nearest whole number.
2 marks
📊 Calculation
Mark Scheme
MP1Correct calculation: SA = 6 × (5²) = 150 mm². Volume = 5³ = 125 mm³. Ratio = 150/125 = 1.2
MP2Rounded to nearest whole number: 82 (this is for a different cube — check paper context; for 5mm cube: 1.2 or value matches actual question)
⚠ The mark scheme answer is 82 — check your paper for the actual cube dimensions. MP1 = correct method and calculation. MP2 = correct rounding. ecf applies from MP1.

Q1(a)(i)
Osmosis — Recording Results in a Table
Context: Investigation of osmosis using dialysis tubing bags in sucrose solution (test-tube S) and distilled water (test-tube W). Measurements taken at 0 and 15 minutes.

Draw a table to record the results for test-tubes S and W at 0 minutes and at 15 minutes.
3 marks
🔴 Table design
Mark Scheme
MP1Table drawn with minimum of 3 columns and a header line
MP2Suitable headings for test-tube AND distance with units in mm or cm
MP3Recording of four correct measurements for both S and W at 0 min and 15 min: S₀ = W₀ = 53.5±2 mm; S₁₅ = 65.0±2 mm; W₁₅ = 53.5±2 mm (approximately unchanged)
⚠ Common errors: no units in headings (-1), table with only 2 columns, missing header line. Test-tube S bag increases in length (water enters by osmosis into sucrose solution). Test-tube W stays the same (equal concentrations).
Q1(b)(iv)
Osmosis — Variables to Keep Constant
Identify two variables that should be kept constant in this osmosis investigation.
2 marks
🔴 Variables — frequently too vague
Mark Scheme — Any 2 from:
✓Volume of water in the test-tubes
✓Volume of liquid in the tubing bag
✓Time dialysis tubing bags left in test-tubes / water-bath
✓Temperature of the water-bath
✓Type / brand of dialysis tubing
✓Where measurements were taken from
⚠ "Same conditions" = 0. Must be specific. "Same temperature" scores, "same environment" does not. Always give measurable, specific variables.
Q2
Transport in Plants — Planning an Investigation
Context: A student investigates the effect of temperature on the rate of transpiration using celery stalks and dye.

Plan an investigation to find out how temperature affects the rate of transpiration. Include: independent variable, dependent variable, method details, variables to keep constant, repetition, and a safety precaution. (6 marks)
6 marks
🔴 6-mark planning — hardest question type
Mark Scheme — 1 mark each:
1Independent variable: at least two different temperatures stated
2Dependent variable: time for dye to reach leaves / a set distance, OR distance dye moves in a set time, OR number of cut sections containing dye
3+4Method details (max 2): how to maintain air temperature at different levels; idea of slicing end off celery before immersion; cutting sections with knife/scalpel
5-7Variables kept constant (max 3): wind speed, humidity, light intensity, length/height of celery, number of leaves/surface area, species/type/age of celery, concentration/volume/colour of dye, time in dye, thickness of cut sections
8Repetition: two or more replicates at each temperature / repeat at least two more times
9Safety precaution: cutting on flat/stable surface; cut away from body; wear gloves/goggles
⚠ A planning question needs ALL components. Forgetting repetition or safety loses easy marks. "Keep everything else the same" for variables = 0; must list specific variables.
Q3(a)
Magnification — Calculating Actual Size
Context: Photograph of a banana borer insect at magnification ×8. Line AB represents the length of the insect.

Measure line AB in Fig. 3.1. Then calculate the actual length of the banana borer using:
magnification = length of line AB ÷ actual length
Give your answer to two significant figures.
3 marks
📊 Sig. figs. calculation
Mark Scheme
MP1Correct measurement of line AB = 90 ± 1 mm
MP2Correct calculation: actual length = 90 ÷ 8 = 11.25 mm → any number of sig figs
MP3Rounded to 2 significant figures: 11 mm
⚠ ecf applies: if measured length is different but calculation method is correct, MP2 and MP3 can still be awarded. Must state answer in mm. 2 sig figs ≠ 2 decimal places.

Plot a bar chart on the grid of this data. (4 marks)
4 marks

Mark Scheme

MP3All bars plotted accurately ± ½ a small square

Q3(b)
Biochemistry — Food Tests (Starch, Protein, Fat)
(i) State the names of the reagents used to test samples of plant tissue for starch and protein.

(ii) Describe the method you would use to test a sample of plant tissue for fat.
2 + 2 marks
⚡ Hard concept — must know all tests
Mark Scheme
StarchIodine solution (iodine in potassium iodide) → turns blue-black if starch present
ProteinBiuret reagent (potassium hydroxide + copper sulfate) → turns purple/violet if protein present
Fat methodAdd ethanol to the sample; shake to dissolve any fat; pour into water; white / cloudy emulsion forms if fat is present
⚠ For the fat test: must state ethanol, then water, then observe white emulsion. Just "ethanol test" = 0. Benedict's reagent tests for reducing sugars (not starch). Biuret uses KOH + CuSO₄ (not just copper sulfate alone).
Q3(c)
Enzymes — Pectinase Investigation Analysis
Context: Table of data: pectinase concentration (%) vs volume of juice (cm³ per g apple). Data: 0.2%→0.69, 0.4%→0.77, 0.5%→0.81, 0.6%→0.85, 0.8%→0.92, 1.0%→0.92

(d)(i) A sample of 150g of apple was placed with 0.8% pectinase. Calculate the volume of juice collected. (1 mark)

(d)(iii) Describe the relationship between volume of juice and pectinase concentration. (2 marks)
3 marks
📊 Calculation + graph description
Mark Scheme
d(i)0.92 cm³/g × 150 g = 138 cm³
d(iii) MP1As pectinase concentration increases, the volume of juice increases (up to 0.8%)
d(iii) MP2Between 0.8% and 1.0%, the volume of juice stays the same / levels off / plateaus (at 0.92 cm³/g)
⚠ Must describe BOTH parts: the increase AND the plateau. Only stating "increases" = 1 mark maximum. Include data values for full credit. The plateau happens because substrate (cell walls) becomes limiting.
Q3(a)(ii)
Magnification — Calculating Actual Diameter
Context: Photograph of persimmon fruit cross-section. Magnification ×2.2. Line PQ represents diameter.

Measure line PQ. Then calculate the actual diameter using:
magnification = length of PQ in photo ÷ actual diameter
Give answer to three significant figures.
3 marks
📊 Sig. figs. calculation
Mark Scheme
MP1Correct measurement of PQ from photo (check actual paper for value)
MP2Correct calculation: actual diameter = measured length ÷ 2.2 (any sig figs)
MP3Answer correctly rounded to 3 significant figures (e.g. 43.6 mm → 43.6; 43.64 → 43.6)
⚠ 3 significant figures ≠ 3 decimal places. Example: 43.636... → 3 sig figs = 43.6. Example: 0.04364 → 3 sig figs = 0.0436 (leading zeros don't count). ecf applies from MP1 to MP2 and MP3.

Q1(a)(ii)
Diffusion — Recording Experimental Results
Context: Investigation of acid diffusion in agar blocks. Different concentrations of acid (1.00, 0.10, 0.01 mol/dm³) used. Heights of yellow region measured in mm.

Draw a table to record the results for all three concentrations. Include column headings with units.
4 marks
🔴 Table design — 4 marks
Mark Scheme
MP1Table drawn with headings underlined AND minimum of two columns/rows
MP2Suitable headings with units: concentration / mol per dm³ AND height (of yellow agar) / mm or cm
MP3Three concentrations and three heights recorded (1.00, 0.10, 0.01)
