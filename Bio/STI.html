Reproduction, STIs & Fertility

Detailed chronological explanation of the video content.

START

Introduction to STIs

Definition: An STI (Sexually Transmitted Infection) is an infection transmitted via body fluids through sexual contact.

The syllabus focuses specifically on HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus).

HIV

HIV Transmission & Mechanism

HIV is a Virus. It is transmitted via exchange of body fluids.

Transmission Methods

  • Unprotected sexual intercourse
  • Sharing needles (drug use)
  • Blood transfusions
  • Mother to child (birth/milk)

The Immune Attack

1. HIV attacks Lymphocytes
2. Less Antibodies are produced
3. Immune system weakens
4. Develops into AIDS
EXAM TRAP: You cannot treat HIV with antibiotics because antibiotics kill bacteria, but HIV is a virus.
MID

Method 1: Natural Birth Control

These methods involve avoiding sex during the fertile window or completely.

Abstinence

Refraining from sex entirely.
100% Effective.

Monitoring

Checking Body Temperature or Cervical Mucus texture to predict ovulation.

Result: Unreliable.

MID

Method 2: Chemical Methods

Using hormones or chemicals to prevent fertilization.

The Contraceptive Pill

Contains Progesterone and Estrogen.

  • Prevents production of FSH (So eggs don't mature).
  • Prevents production of LH (So ovulation doesn't happen).

IUD (Intrauterine Device)

Inserted into uterus. Prevents the embryo from implanting in the lining.

Spermicide

Chemical that kills sperm. Usually used with barrier methods.

IMP

Method 3: Mechanical (Barrier)

Physical barriers that stop sperm meeting egg.

  • Condom (Male)
  • Femidom (Female Condom)
  • Diaphragm (Cap over cervix)
CRITICAL POINT: The Condom is the ONLY method of birth control that also prevents the spread of STIs like HIV.
END

Method 4: Surgical

Permanent sterilization procedures.

Vasectomy (Men)

Cutting and tying the Sperm Ducts.

Sperm cannot leave testes.

Tubal Ligation (Women)

Cutting and tying the Oviducts.

Egg cannot reach uterus.

IVF

Fertility Treatments

When couples cannot conceive naturally.

1. Artificial Insemination

Used for low sperm count. Sperm is injected directly into the female.

2. IVF (In Vitro Fertilization)

"In Glass" fertilization.

  1. Mother takes Fertility Drugs (FSH/LH) to mature multiple eggs.
  2. Eggs are collected from ovaries. Sperm is collected.
  3. Fertilization happens in a petri dish (lab).
  4. Eggs develop into Embryos.
  5. Embryos are implanted into mother's uterus.
Social Implications (Syllabus Requirement):
1. Very Expensive.
2. Increases risk of multiple births (twins/triplets).
3. Ethical issues regarding unused embryos.
