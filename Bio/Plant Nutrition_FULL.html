Plant Nutrition

Plants are autotrophs; they make their own organic nutrients (carbohydrates) from simple inorganic raw materials (carbon dioxide and water) using light energy. This chapter covers photosynthesis, leaf anatomy, and environmental factors.

1. Photosynthesis

Definition:
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants manufacture carbohydrates from raw materials using energy from light.

The Equations

You must memorize both the word and chemical equations.

  • Word Equation:
    Carbon Dioxide + Water → (light + chlorophyll) → Glucose + Oxygen
  • Balanced Chemical Equation:
    6CO2 + 6H2O → C6H12O6 + 6O2

Chlorophyll is the green pigment in chloroplasts that absorbs light energy. This energy is used to convert CO2 and H2O into glucose. Oxygen is released as a by-product.

Use of Products

ProductFate / Use
GlucoseUsed for respiration (energy), converted to sucrose for transport, or converted to cellulose for cell walls.
StarchGlucose is converted to starch for storage. Starch is insoluble, so it does not affect the water potential (osmosis) of the cell.
Amino AcidsGlucose + Nitrates → Amino Acids → Proteins (for growth).
OxygenUsed in respiration or excreted via stomata.

2. Leaf Structure & Adaptations

Leaves are adapted to maximize photosynthesis (absorbing light and gas exchange).

StructureAdaptation/Function
Waxy CuticleWaterproof layer on top; prevents water loss (evaporation) but allows light through.
Upper EpidermisTransparent cells (no chloroplasts) to let light pass to the palisade layer.
Palisade MesophyllPacked with many chloroplasts. Located near the top to absorb maximum light. Main site of photosynthesis.
Spongy MesophyllLoose cells with air spaces to allow gas exchange (CO2 in, O2 out). Contains fewer chloroplasts.
Vascular BundleContains Xylem (transports water/minerals) and Phloem (transports sugars/amino acids).
StomataPores on the underside of the leaf for gas diffusion.
Guard CellsControl the opening and closing of stomata.

3. Mineral Nutrition

Plants need ions for healthy growth. The two most important for IGCSE are:

Mineral IonFunctionDeficiency Symptom
Nitrate IonsRequired to make amino acids (for proteins).Stunted growth; weak stem.
Magnesium IonsRequired to make chlorophyll.Yellowing between veins (Chlorosis).

4. Limiting Factors

Definition: Limiting Factor
Something present in the environment in such short supply that it restricts life processes.

The rate of photosynthesis can be limited by:

  1. Light Intensity: As light increases, rate increases until it plateaus (another factor becomes limiting).
  2. Carbon Dioxide Concentration: As CO2 increases, rate increases until it plateaus.
  3. Temperature: Photosynthesis uses enzymes. Rate increases with temperature up to an optimum, then drops rapidly as enzymes denature.
Exam Warning: Glasshouses
In glasshouses (greenhouses), farmers control these factors to maximize yield. They use artificial lights, burn gas to add CO2, and use heaters/ventilation to maintain optimum temperature.

5. Practical Experiments

A. Testing a Leaf for Starch

This proves photosynthesis has occurred.

  1. Boil leaf in water: Kills the leaf, stops enzymes, breaks cell membranes.
  2. Boil in ethanol: Removes chlorophyll (decolorizes leaf). Safety: Turn off Bunsen burner (ethanol is flammable).
  3. Dip in hot water: Softens the brittle leaf.
  4. Add Iodine Solution:
    • Positive result (Starch present): Blue-Black.
    • Negative result: Orange-Brown.

B. Requirement Tests

  • Light: Cover part of a leaf with foil. Destarch plant first. Expose to light. Test for starch. Only exposed part turns blue-black.
  • Chlorophyll: Use a variegated leaf (white and green patches). Only green parts turn blue-black.
  • CO2: Enclose leaf in a flask with Soda Lime (absorbs CO2). Leaf will produce no starch.

C. Rate of Photosynthesis (Pondweed)

Count bubbles of oxygen produced by aquatic plants (e.g., Elodea) over time.

  • Independent Variable: Distance of lamp (Light Intensity).
  • Dependent Variable: Number of bubbles per minute (Rate).
  • Control Variables: Temperature (use water bath), CO2 (add sodium hydrogencarbonate).
