Reproduction in Plants

Reproduction is the process that makes more of the same kind of organism. Plants can reproduce asexually (cloning) or sexually (using gametes). This guide covers flower anatomy, pollination, fertilization, and seed germination.

1. Types of Reproduction

FeatureAsexual ReproductionSexual Reproduction
ParentsOne parent.Two parents (usually).
GametesNo gametes (sex cells) involved.Involves fusion of male and female gametes.
OffspringGenetically identical (Clones).Genetically different (Variation).
ProcessMitosis only.Meiosis (gametes) and Mitosis (growth).
ExamplesPotato tubers, Strawberry runners.Flowering plants, Humans.

2. Anatomy of a Flower

Flowers are the reproductive organs of plants. They usually contain both male and female parts (hermaphrodite).

  • Sepals: Protect the flower bud before it opens.
  • Petals: Often bright and scented to attract insects.
  • Stamen (Male): Consists of the Anther (produces pollen) and the Filament (supports anther).
  • Carpel (Female): Consists of the Stigma (collects pollen), Style (connects stigma to ovary), and Ovary (contains ovules).

3. Pollination

Definition: The transfer of pollen grains from the anther to the stigma.

Self-pollination: Pollen transferred within the same flower or to another flower on the same plant. (Reduces variation).

Cross-pollination: Pollen transferred to a flower on a different plant of the same species. (Increases variation).

Insect vs. Wind Pollination

FeatureInsect-PollinatedWind-Pollinated
PetalsLarge, brightly colored.Small, dull (green/brown), or absent.
Scent/NectarPresent (to attract insects).Absent.
PollenSticky or spiky (clumps together). Small amount.Light, smooth, and dry. Huge amounts.
AnthersInside the flower (stiff).Hang outside loosely (to catch wind).
StigmaInside flower, sticky (to wipe pollen off insect).Hang outside, feathery (to catch drifting pollen).

4. Fertilization

Pollination is just the arrival of pollen. Fertilization is the fusion of the male gamete nucleus with the female gamete nucleus.

The Process:
  1. Pollen grain lands on a compatible stigma.
  2. A pollen tube grows down the style, digesting tissue as it goes.
  3. The tube enters the ovary through the micropyle.
  4. The male nucleus travels down the tube and fuses with the female nucleus in the ovule.
  5. A zygote is formed.

Seed and Fruit Formation

After fertilization:

  • The Ovule becomes the Seed.
  • The Ovary becomes the Fruit.
  • Petals and sepals usually fall off.

5. Germination

Germination is the start of growth in the seed. The seed contains an embryo (shoot/root) and a food store (cotyledon).

Exam Tip: Conditions for Germination
Remember WOW. Seeds do NOT need light or soil to start germinating.
1. Water: To activate enzymes and swell the seed.
2. Oxygen: For respiration (energy to grow).
3. Warmth: Optimum temperature for enzyme activity.
