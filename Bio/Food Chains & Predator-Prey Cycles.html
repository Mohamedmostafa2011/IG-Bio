Predator-Prey Cycles

2:41

Populations do not remain static; they have a dynamic relationship where they influence one another in a continuous cycle.

Phase 1 (3:41) Predators are Low: With fewer predators, the prey population begins to increase rapidly due to reduced predation.
Phase 2 (4:00) Prey is Abundant: The high number of prey provides ample food, causing the predator population to rise.
Phase 3 (4:11) Predators are High: As predators peak, they consume more prey, causing the prey population to crash.
Phase 4 (4:24) Prey is Low: With food scarce, the predator population eventually declines, restarting the cycle.
Key Concept: The Time Lag

The cycle is Out of Phase. Predator numbers lag behind prey numbers because it takes time for populations to reproduce and grow (4:40).
