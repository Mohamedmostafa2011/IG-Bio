Reproduction in Humans

Human reproduction is sexual, involving the fusion of male and female gametes (sperm and egg) to create genetically distinct offspring. This chapter covers anatomy, gametes, fertilization, pregnancy, the menstrual cycle, and STIs.

1. Reproductive Systems

The Male Reproductive System

PartFunction
TestesProduce sperm and the hormone testosterone.
ScrotumSac that holds the testes outside the body to keep them cooler (optimum temp for sperm production).
Sperm DuctTransports sperm from the testes to the urethra.

UrethraTube carrying urine or semen out of the body.

The Female Reproductive System

PartFunction

3. Fertilization & Development

Key Definition: Fertilization
The fusion of the nuclei of the male gamete (sperm) and the female gamete (egg) to form a zygote. This produces a diploid cell (46 chromosomes).
  1. Ovulation: An egg is released from the ovary into the oviduct.
  2. Copulation: Sperm is deposited in the vagina.
  3. Fertilization: Happens in the oviduct. The zygote forms.
  4. Implantation: The zygote divides by mitosis to form a ball of cells (embryo) which sinks into the uterus lining.

The Placenta and Amniotic Fluid

The fetus connects to the placenta via the Umbilical Cord.

  • Placenta: Allows exchange of materials. Oxygen and nutrients (glucose/amino acids) move from Mother → Fetus. CO2 and Urea move from Fetus → Mother.
    Note: It also acts as a barrier to most pathogens, but viruses (Rubella/HIV) and drugs (Nicotine/Alcohol) can cross it.
  • Amniotic Sac/Fluid: A membrane enclosing the fetus containing fluid. It protects the fetus from mechanical shock and drying out.
Exam Warning: Blood Mixing
The mother's blood and fetal blood DO NOT MIX. They are separated by a thin wall in the placenta. Mixing would cause clotting due to different blood types and high maternal blood pressure would damage the fetus.

4. The Menstrual Cycle

The cycle lasts about 28 days. It prepares the uterus for pregnancy.

  • Days 1-5 (Menstruation): Uterus lining breaks down (period).
  • Days 6-13: Lining rebuilds.
  • Day 14 (Ovulation): Egg is released.
  • Days 15-28: Lining maintained. If no fertilization, cycle repeats.

5. Puberty & Secondary Characteristics

Controlled by Testosterone (Male) and Oestrogen (Female).

  • Males: Voice breaks (deepens), muscle growth, facial/body hair, sperm production starts.
  • Females: Breasts develop, hips widen, menstruation begins, body hair.

6. STIs (Sexually Transmitted Infections)

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus): Attacks lymphocytes (white blood cells), leading to AIDS (Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome). The body cannot fight other infections.

  • Transmission: Unprotected sex, sharing needles, blood transfusions, mother to child (placenta/breastmilk).
  • Prevention: Use condoms (barrier method), sterile needles, screening blood.
