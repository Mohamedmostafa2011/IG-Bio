VECTORS

Disease Transmission Carriers

Vectors are organisms that play a crucial role in the transmission of infectious diseases. Pathogens, on the other hand, are the actual causative agents of disease, such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protozoa. Vectors serve as "carriers" in a more mechanical sense, allowing pathogens to move from one host to another.

🔄 TRANSMISSION CYCLE

🧬 Host A
➜
🦟 Vector
➜
🔬 Multiply
➜
🎯 Host B

⚡ KEY STEPS

1

A vector ingests a pathogen from one host, which then establishes itself within the vector's body.

2

The pathogen multiplies within the vector, increasing its chances of being transmitted to a new host.

3

When the vector bites or feeds on another host, it regurgitates or defecates, releasing the pathogens into the new host.
