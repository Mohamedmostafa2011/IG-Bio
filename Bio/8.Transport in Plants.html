2. Water Uptake

Water enters through Root Hair Cells.

  • Root Hairs: Large surface area for absorption.
  • Mechanism: Water enters by Osmosis (soil has higher water potential than cell cytoplasm).
  • Pathway: Root hair → Root cortex cells → Xylem → Leaf mesophyll.

3. Transpiration

Definition: Loss of water vapour from plant leaves by evaporation of water at the surfaces of the mesophyll cells followed by diffusion of water vapour through the stomata.

Transpiration Pull: Water is cohesive (sticks together). As water evaporates from the leaf, it pulls the column of water up the xylem.

Factors Affecting Transpiration Rate:

  • Temperature: Higher temp → Faster evaporation → Faster rate.
  • Humidity: Higher humidity → Lower concentration gradient → Slower rate.
  • Wind Speed: Higher wind → Removes vapour around leaf → Faster rate.
  • Light Intensity: Higher light → Stomata open → Faster rate.

4. Translocation

Definition: The movement of sucrose and amino acids in phloem:
- From regions of production (Source)
- To regions of storage or utilization (Sink).

Example: In summer, Leaf (Source) → Root (Sink). In spring, Root (Source) → Growing Bud (Sink).
